Charles-Augustin de Coulomb

an electrifying figure in the world of science

a time line of the life of de Couloumb

  • 1736 - born in Angouleme, France
  • 1761 - graduates from a military academy and joins the French armed forces as a lieutenant engineer
  • 1764 - leads the team that the builds Fort Bourbon in the West Indies
  • 1779 - publishes Theorie des machines simples a foundational treatise on the laws of friction
  • 1785 - publishes several papers that explain the that the attraction or repulsion of charges is proportional to the amounts of charge and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the charges
  • 1789 - retires and moves to his estate in Blois
  • 1806 - dies in Paris, France
